Time Telling Game

Game Engine: Unity
Language: C#
Platform: Web Browser
Date of completion: 3/10/23
Twinkl Go! Page: Link

The Time Telling Game involves teaching children from Key Stage 2 how to read both analogue and digital clocks as well as converting 12 hours format into 24 hours and vice-versa. Both the analogue and digital clocks are adjusted differently from each other. The analogue clock requires the player to drag and hold the minute hand to adjust the time while the digital clock requires the player to just press the up and down arrow buttons to adjust the hour and minutes. There are 12 questions the player must answer correctly to win the game.

- The game randomly generates the correct answer for the question and the player has to adjust the clock depending on the type of question such as if the question is asking the player to convert analogue to digital or vice-versa.
- Other question formats like o'clock, half-past, and quarter hours like 15, 30, 45 minutes are expressed in words for example, "Set the clock to half-past twelve".
- The minute hand from the analogue clock has both a minimum and maximum threshold for the correct answer when it comes to the question to give players leniency.
- The background shown outside the window of the game changes colour depending on the time on the analogue to represent the different times of the day to teach children the different stages of day and night time.

Picnic Antics - Six Times Table Remake

Game Engine: Unity
Language: C#
Platform: Web Browser
Date of completion: 24/5/23
Twinkl Go! Page: Link

Picnic Antics was an old maths game created by a previous intern in Twinkl many years ago and I was tasked to remake this game as the previous version of the game played very slowly. So I decided to design the game to flow much faster during the gameplay loop. The purpose of this game is to teach children six times tables. The game presents a sum from the six times tables and the player must select a food item with the correct answer on it. Once the player selects a food item, it summons four ants to pick up the food and give it to the ant presenting the sum. The game is completed once the player completes all 12 questions from the six times tables.

- The game randomly generates a sum from the six times tables with the answer on one of the random food items with numbers.
- Once the player selects a food item, four ants are spawned from the corners of the screen and pick up the food item to give to the ant with the sum.
- The game checks if the selected food item is correct with the presented sum. If the player is correct, the ant shows a smile emoticon and another random sum appears while the food items are randomized with different sprites and numbers with one of the numbers being the correct answer for the sum.

Circuit Components Game

Game Engine: Unity
Language: C#
Platform: Web Browser
Date of completion: 1/9/22
Twinkl Go! Page: Link

The Circuits Components Game is an interactive science-based game where the player must follow the diagram on the right-hand side of the screen and create the correct circuit to advance through the game. The game progressively gets harder when bigger circuits are required to be made with much more components. The characters Etta and Granbot are original characters Twinkl have created for science-related topics and subjects like science animations. This game is helpful for schools that do not have the necessary equipment to teach children about electricity and how components work.

- Every circuit requires a power source(such as a cell or battery) to flow power through a circuit that are connected to components which is why I created a script that checks all components that are connected through a power source.
- The script recursively checks through all of the components that are connected with each other and once the algorithm identifies that the start and end of the connection is the same power source then the game recognises that the circuit is completely connected.
- Once a fully connected circuit is complete, the game checks if the connected circuit is created with the correct components. If the circuit is correct, both Etta and Granbot appear and play a success animation. Once that happens, the game progresses and a new circuit diagram is revealed.

Sudoku

Game Engine: Unity
Language: C#
Platform: Web Browser
Date of completion: 29/4/22
Twinkl Go! Page: Link

This was my first ever project for Twinkl Go when I first started. I worked with a Product Owner who was in charge of a section in Twinkl called the News Room which provides delivering news in a kid-friendly way. I was provided with a resource specification document which explained to me what was required for this game such as having a 4x4 layout and number boxes from 1 to 4 to be dragged into the empty boxes on the screen.

- The 4x4 grid was intialized as an array which stores information about the grid such as the position and number. The 2x2 mini-grid was postioned differently when the whole 4x4 grid was being generated.
- The sudoku board is generated with numbers from 1 to 4, picking a random position in the mini-grids and checking if that box is a valid position to place that number. If not, then re-randomize a position in the board and check again if that box is valid to place a number.
- Once the numbers have been placed in the board, the algorithm checks if the current grid can be completed. If the grid can be completed then randomly remove most of the numbers. If not, then remake the entire board.
- The reason for creating the algorithm this way is because the solution must be generated first before removing most of the numbers in the board. The algorithm must check beforehand if the number generation has a solution. Otherwise, the sudoku puzzle is impossible to complete by the player.

Infinite Defender

Game Engine: Unity
Language: C#
Platform: Android devices
Date of completion: 7/7/17
Unity WebGL Player: Link
Source Code: Link

Infinite Defender is a throwback to the old-fashioned arcade space-shooting games with a twist. Enemy waves are procedurally-generated so they spawn differently every time you play it. The waves continuously spawn until the player dies so survive as long as you can and earn a high score. This 2D project was made solo and it is intended for the Android devices and smartphones.

- The moving and shooting at 00:06 was made to move the ship towards where the player has touched on the screen. If the player touched on the left side of the screen then the ship moves towards the left. If the player touched the right side of the screen then the ship moves towards the right.
- The enemies spawning in at 00:13 had movement in a shape of an arc which was made by using a function from transform called RotateAround.
- At 00:37, the enemies that move in a circular motion was achieved by using the Cosine and Sin math functions.
- At 1:04, enemies have a small chance of dropping a random power-up that can boost the player's attributes such as: attack speed, movement speed, defense, shield up, and base health up. The ship icon with a shield around it coloured green is the shield up boost.

Lemuria: Land of Lost Treasures

Game Engine: Unity
Language: C#
Platform: Android devices
Date of completion: 24/1/17
Unity WebGL Player: Link
Source Code: Link

This is a 2D adventure game where the player has to explore around the world to collect rare treasures and collectibles. The challenges contained in this game are grid-based. This project was made by four people. This game was intended for Android devices as well as smartphones.

- The sokoban puzzle at 00:11 was procedurally generated by carving out the level area with using 3 by 3 templates with different layouts to differentiate the area.
- The spike trap maze at 00:35 was created with using a Depth First Search algorithm to create a maze with no deadends to provide multiple paths for the player to reach the exit. The spikes were randomly generated in the pathway and the algorithm tunneled out the maze when the level area is intially generated with walls.
- The doppelganger event at 1:11 had a sequence puzzle where you have to match the flashing symbols on the ground with the doppelganger in order. The doppelganger moves opposite of your character.
- The combat at 1:26 is grid-based where the player has to move the character's attack range indicated by the red boxes onto the enemy to attack and defeat the monster.

This game was created by 7 members for a group project during university. Lunar Odyssey is a 2D endless survival game where the player has to avoid obstacles created by the Greek gods. Use the mouse to drag the moon vertically up and down to control the ocean waves and drag the boat left to right would move the boat horizontally. I have used what I have learned from the module called Games Studio to implement obstacles by using Unity components such as:

- The volcano spewing up fiery rocks at 0:49 had the rigidbody component attached to it to fall from the sky and collide with the ship which causes damage if it hits.
- The cyclops picking up the boulder and throwing it above himself at 1:40 uses the rigidbody component to animate the boulder being picked up and thrown at the ship would collide with the ship if it hits.
- The kraken's tentacle attack at 2:27 has both a collider and a rigidbody component which can cause damage if the tentacles hit the ship.
- The lightning cloud at spawned at 3:18 has a particle effect with a lightning texture attached to it which can collide with the ship.
